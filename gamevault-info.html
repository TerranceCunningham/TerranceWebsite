GameVault E-Commerce Studies.

In my E-Commerce class I decided to create a video game store. GameVault was a fun challenge for me as the images and look all came from inspiration while researching different types of video games and color schemes. Vivid spotlight images, Detailed discriptions for products and Easy navigation make customers visits at GameVault user friendly.View Site

INFORMATION ARCHETCTURE

Project: GameVault Information Architecture and Interaction Design used to map out the structure of site.

GameVault Information Architecture included many ways to buy new or old games, and provided options to buy accessories items for your gaming system. The Information Architecture needed to include a way for clients to add items to a shopping cart and provide purchasing choices.

COLOR SCHEME

Project: GameVault Color Scheme Studies.

Researching GameVaults Color Scheme I choose light and suttle colors, The images that I choose are very bright and loud so the mixture of both make it easy on your eyes.

WIREFRAMING

Project: GameVault Wireframes and Check Out Studies.

The GameVault site included eight different pages of which a 12 column grid was used as the base structure. GameVault's main page was design to capture the customer's eye with a vivid spotlight showing the latest selection of games, I designed the website so that customers are encouraged and can easily purchase accessories for their gaming systems.

VISUAL DESIGN

Project: GameVault Look And Feel.

Adding light greys to the dark of GameVaults Color Scheme it makes it easy on the customers eyes. As the images I created pertray the site the customer will feel right at home to check out the latest games and accesseries.View Site
