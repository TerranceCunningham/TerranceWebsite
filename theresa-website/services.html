Services

Screening of Patient: when visiting a facility or establishing the patient's need for dental hygiene services, a screening is necessary to determine the patients dental history and establish if mobile dental hygiene services is in the best interest of the patient. Assessment of Patient: assessing a patient's oral hygiene care and gingival condition is necessary to establish the interval of preventive services, daily oral hygiene regiment, and evaluation of preventive services. Adult Prophylaxis: a "dental cleaning" for ages 13+ with minimal calculus and stain on their teeth.Child Prophylaxis: a "dental cleaning" for ages 0-12 Fluoride Varnish Application: a procedure that places fluoride on the teeth that help to maintain the strength and integrity of the tooth structure and decrease the chances of decay Oral Hygiene Instructions: a tailored plan to each patient that establishes their brushing and flossing habits, their oral hygiene care protocol and discussion on patient's needs on a daily basis Sealant: a procedure that places a protective coating of plastic filling material into the pit and grooves on the biting surfaces of teeth that protect this surface from decay Scaling and Root Planing: a "deep" cleaning that involves cleaning the roots of teeth and treating for periodontal disease.

Periodontal Maintenance: a cleaning that is done after scale and rootplaning to maintain the cleanliness of the root pockets of the teeth. Usually this cleaning is done every 3-4 months. Behavioral Management Report: a report completed that discusses the behavioral management of a patient. Smooth/polished restoration: a procedure that polishes an existing restoration or smooths a fractured filling to stop causing harm to a patient's cheek lip or tongue.
